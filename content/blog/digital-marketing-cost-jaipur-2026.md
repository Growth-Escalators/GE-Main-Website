## What actually drives the cost

Before the numbers, the variables that move them. Two businesses buying "SEO" can pay 4x different prices and both be paying fairly, because:

- **Scope of work.** One channel (say, just Meta ads management) costs less than a full-stack retainer covering SEO, ads, social, and content.
- **Category competitiveness.** Real estate, healthcare, and legal are expensive categories to rank or advertise in — more competitors bidding on the same keywords and audiences. A local bakery's SEO retainer and a real estate builder's SEO retainer are not the same job.
- **Team seniority and delivery model.** A solo freelancer, a junior execution shop, and a senior in-house pod all price differently — and deliver differently.
- **Ad spend size.** Managing ₹50k/month in ad spend takes a fraction of the optimization hours that managing ₹15L/month does. Fees usually scale with spend, not stay flat.
- **Contract length.** Month-to-month retainers cost more per month than 6- or 12-month commitments, because the agency is pricing in churn risk.
- **Reporting and tooling overhead.** Real-time dashboards, server-side tracking, and AI lead-scoring cost more to run than a monthly PDF.

## Retainer vs. project pricing

Most digital marketing spend falls into two structures, and picking the wrong one wastes money:

- **Project pricing** fits one-off, scoped work with a clear end state — a website rebuild, a brand shoot, a one-time SEO audit. You pay once, you own the output.
- **Retainer pricing** fits ongoing, compounding work — SEO, ads management, content, social. These channels need continuous optimization; a "build it and walk away" project structure doesn't work because rankings and ad performance decay without upkeep.

If an agency tries to sell you a one-time "SEO project" with no ongoing component, be skeptical — SEO is a retainer discipline, not a deliverable you finish.

## Service fees vs. ad spend — don't confuse the two

The single most common pricing confusion: treating the agency fee and the ad spend as the same number.

Example: a ₹75,000/month management fee sitting on top of ₹2,00,000/month in Meta and Google ad spend means your *total* monthly marketing outlay is ₹2,75,000 — the fee is roughly 27% of spend. That's within the normal 15–25% management-fee-to-spend range for accounts under ₹5L/month (the percentage typically drops as spend scales, since optimization effort doesn't grow linearly with budget).

If an agency quotes you "₹75,000/month for digital marketing" without clarifying whether that includes ad spend, ask directly. It's the most common way founders get a pricing surprise in month two.

## How to set your budget by revenue stage

A rough but workable rule: growth-stage businesses spend **5–10% of revenue** on marketing, split between service fees and ad spend, with the mix shifting as you scale.

- **Under ₹50L annual revenue:** Test one channel properly before spreading thin. ₹25k–₹50k/month total (fees + spend) is realistic.
- **₹50L–₹3Cr annual revenue:** ₹75k–₹2L/month total, split roughly 30% fees / 70% spend once you've found a working channel.
- **₹3Cr–₹15Cr annual revenue:** ₹2L–₹8L/month total, multi-channel, with dedicated account management justified.
- **Above ₹15Cr:** Custom-quoted — spend and fee structure should be built around unit economics (CAC, LTV, payback period), not a generic percentage.

## Red flags of cheap agencies

A low quote isn't automatically bad — but these patterns usually mean the "savings" are coming out of your results:

- **No named account lead** — you get a rotating cast of juniors instead of one person accountable for outcomes.
- **Vague fee-vs-spend breakdown** — if they can't tell you in one sentence how much of your money is fee vs. platform spend, walk away.
- **Identical packages regardless of industry** — a real estate builder and a local café should not get the same "Silver/Gold/Platinum" package.
- **Guaranteed rankings or lead counts** — nobody controls Google's algorithm or a market's total demand. Guarantees like this are a sales tactic, not a capability.
- **Long lock-in with no exit clause** — a confident agency doesn't need a 12-month contract with cancellation penalties to keep you.
- **Reporting that's a PDF, not a live number** — if you can't check performance yourself between calls, you're being managed by the agency's narrative, not your data.
