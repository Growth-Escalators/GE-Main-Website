Picking a digital marketing agency in Jaipur usually happens one of two ways: you go with whoever gave the slickest pitch, or whoever quoted the lowest number. Both are bad selection criteria, and both are why so many businesses cycle through two or three agencies in as many years without finding one that sticks.

This isn't another ranked list of agencies — you can find those elsewhere. This is a buyer's framework: the 10 questions that surface whether an agency can actually deliver, asked *before* you've signed a contract and handed over a budget.

## Why the wrong agency costs more than a bad hire

A bad employee costs you a notice period. A bad agency costs you a locked-in contract, months of ad spend that never should have gone out the door, and the ranking positions, pixel history, and creative library you now have to rebuild from zero with whoever comes next.

The stakes are higher than most owners treat them, yet these 10 questions take 20 minutes on a discovery call. A failed six-month engagement typically costs ₹3–8L in wasted fees and misdirected spend — the questions are the cheapest insurance you'll buy this year.

## The 10 questions to ask before you sign

### 1. Who actually does the work on my account?
Every agency pitch is run by a senior salesperson or founder. Ask specifically who executes: is it the person across from you, or does the account get handed to a junior team once the contract is signed? Get the actual names and experience level of the strategist, media buyer, and content person who'll touch your account week to week.

### 2. What's your reporting cadence — and can I see it live?
"You'll get a monthly report" is a soft answer. Ask whether you get a live dashboard you can check anytime, or whether you're waiting on a scheduled PDF. Real-time visibility into spend and performance is table stakes for any agency running meaningful ad budgets in 2026 — if they can't offer it, ask why not.

### 3. What's the contract length, and what does it cost to leave early?
Some lock-in is normal — an agency needs a few months to show results. But a 12-month contract with a stiff cancellation penalty usually signals an agency optimizing for retention through friction rather than performance. Ask for the exact rupee number, not "we'll work something out."

### 4. Do I own the ad accounts, pixels, and assets — or do you?
This is the single most consequential question on the list. Ad accounts, Meta pixels, Google Tag Manager containers, landing pages, and your content library should all live inside *your* business's own accounts, with you holding admin access. If the agency holds these under its own umbrella instead, you have zero leverage to leave — and you'll lose your pixel's learning history the day you try.

### 5. Can you show me a real case study, not a testimonial screenshot?
A testimonial quote is easy to write. A real case study has a starting baseline, the specific actions taken, a timeframe, and a verifiable result. Ask for one in your industry, or a comparable one, with numbers you can sanity-check — and ask if you can speak to the client directly.

### 6. What's your pricing model, exactly?
Flat management fee, percentage of ad spend, or a bundled package with no line-item breakdown? Any of these can be fair — but the agency should explain, in one sentence, what share of your money is fee versus platform spend. If they can't, that ambiguity is doing work for them, not for you.

### 7. Do you guarantee rankings or a specific number of leads?
This is a trick question — the correct answer is no. No agency controls Google's algorithm or a market's total search demand. An agency promising "page 1 in 90 days" or "50 leads a month, guaranteed" is selling a promise it can't keep, and the fine print usually protects the agency, not you.

### 8. Who's my single point of contact, and how often do we actually talk?
Growing accounts get passed around inside agencies. Confirm there's one named account lead accountable for your results — not a rotating cast of juniors who each know a third of your account's history.

### 9. Have you actually run campaigns in my industry, recently?
Compliance, buyer psychology, and unit economics differ enormously between a healthcare clinic and a D2C skincare brand. Ask for a recent, relevant example — not a five-year-old case study from a category they no longer serve.

### 10. What happens if I want to leave in 90 days?
Ask this before you sign, not after you're unhappy. A confident agency walks you through exactly what you keep — accounts, pixels, content, audience data — and how handover works. A vague "we'll figure it out" here is the clearest predictor of a painful exit later.

## Red flags of cheap, churn-heavy agencies

A low quote isn't disqualifying on its own — but these patterns, especially stacked together, usually mean the savings are coming out of your results:

- **No named account lead** — you're managed by "the team," which means nobody specifically
- **Identical packages regardless of your business** — a bakery and a builder get the same "Gold Plan"
- **Vague or bundled pricing** — can't separate fee from ad spend in one sentence
- **Guaranteed outcomes** — rankings, lead counts, or ROAS promised in the pitch itself
- **Assets created under the agency's own accounts**, not yours
- **A 12-month contract with no performance checkpoint or clean exit clause**
- **Reporting that's a static PDF**, not something you can check yourself between calls
- **References that are old case studies, not current clients** you can actually call

## The 10-question checklist

Use this on your next discovery call and score the agency honestly.

| # | Question | Strong answer | Red-flag answer |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Who does the work? | Named strategist, media buyer, content lead | "Our team" — no names given |
| 2 | Reporting cadence? | Live dashboard, checkable anytime | Monthly PDF only |
| 3 | Contract length? | 3–6 months, clear cancellation terms | 12 months, stiff penalty |
| 4 | Who owns the assets? | You, in your own business accounts | Agency-owned accounts |
| 5 | Case studies? | Verifiable, with baseline and numbers | Testimonial quotes only |
| 6 | Pricing model? | Clear fee-vs-spend split | Bundled, unclear split |
| 7 | Guarantees offered? | No guarantees, honest ranges | "Guaranteed" rankings or leads |
| 8 | Point of contact? | One named account lead | Rotating juniors |
| 9 | Industry experience? | Recent, relevant example | Old or unrelated case study |
| 10 | Exit terms? | Clear handover, you keep everything | "We'll figure it out" |

If an agency scores a red flag on four or more of these, keep looking — no price discount makes up for that combination.
